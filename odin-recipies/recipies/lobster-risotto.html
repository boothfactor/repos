Lobster Risotto

Gordon Ramsay's Hell's Kitchen Lobster Risotto is a luxurious and flavorful dish that combines succulent lobster with creamy risotto. The lobster adds a rich and sweet flavor to the dish, while the risotto is cooked to perfection, creating a creamy and velvety texture. This dish is perfect for special occasions or when you want to impress your guests with a gourmet meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 Lobsters
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 Shallots, Finely Chopped
  • 1.5 cups Arborio Rice
  • 1/2 cup White Wine
  • 4 cumps Hot Chicken Stock
  • 1/2 cup Grated Parmesan Cheese
  • Salt And Pepper To Taste
  • Fresh Parsley or Chives for Garnish

Directions

  1. Start by preparing the lobster. Remove the meat from the shell and chop it into bite-sized pieces.
  2. In a large pan, melt butter and saut√© finely chopped shallots until translucent.
  3. Add Arborio rice to the pan and stir it for a minute until it is coated in butter.
  4. Pour in white wine and let it simmer until it is absorbed by the rice.
  5. Gradually add hot chicken stock to the pan, stirring constantly until the rice absorbs the liquid.
  6. Continue adding stock and stirring until the rice is cooked al dente.
  7. Stir in the chopped lobster meat and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the lobster is heated through.
  8. Finish the risotto by stirring in grated Parmesan cheese and a knob of butter for extra creaminess
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Serve the lobster risotto hot, garnished with fresh parsley or chives.

Cooking Tips

  • To extract maximum flavor from the lobster shells, simmer them in the chicken stock before using it in the risotto.
  • Make sure to stir the risotto constantly to prevent it from sticking to the pan and to achieve a creamy texture.
  • If you prefer a more intense lobster flavor, you can add a small amount of lobster stock or lobster base to the risotto.

Storage and Serving

  • Leftover lobster risotto can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
  • Reheat the risotto gently in a pan with a splash of chicken stock or water to maintain its creamy texture.
  • Serve the lobster risotto as a main course with a side salad or as a side dish with grilled seafood or steak.
